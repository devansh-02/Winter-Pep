History of Cricket

Cricket has a rich history dating back to the 16th century in England. The game evolved from a simple pastime to an organized sport with the establishment of the Marylebone Cricket Club (MCC) in 1787. It quickly spread to other parts of the British Empire, making it one of the most loved sports globally.

Cricket Formats

Cricket is played in several formats, catering to diverse audiences:

  • Test Cricket: The longest format, played over five days.
  • One-Day Internationals (ODI): Limited to 50 overs per side, focusing on fast-paced action.
  • T20 Cricket: The shortest format, with 20 overs per side, known for high-energy matches.

Cricket Legends

Many players have left an indelible mark on the game. Here are some cricket legends:

  • Sir Don Bradman: Known as the greatest batsman of all time.
  • Sachin Tendulkar: The "God of Cricket" with countless records.
  • MS Dhoni: Famous for his calm demeanor and incredible captaincy.
  • Virat Kohli: A modern-day legend known for his consistency and aggression.
